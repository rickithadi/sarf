# How Our Surf Forecast Works

Our forecasting system uses scientifically-backed calculations based on oceanographic standards and surf forecasting best practices to provide you with meaningful data about wave quality and conditions.

---

## Understanding Wave Height

### Significant Wave Height (Hs)

When we talk about wave height, we use **Significant Wave Height (Hs)** - the standard measurement used by meteorologists and oceanographers worldwide.

**What it means:** Hs is the average height of the highest one-third of waves. This closely matches what an experienced observer would estimate when looking at the ocean.

**In practice:**
- When we say "1m waves", expect a mix of smaller waves with occasional larger sets
- About 1 in 7 waves will be larger than the forecast height
- Set waves can be up to 1.5-2x the forecast height

### Surf Range

We display wave height as a range (e.g., "3-4ft") because waves don't come in uniform sizes. The range accounts for:
- Natural wave variability
- Swell organization (ground swell vs wind swell)
- Wave steepness

## Why Wave Period Matters

**Wave period** is the time in seconds between successive wave crests. It's one of the most important factors for surf quality.

### Period Quality Guide

| Period | Type | What to Expect |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **14s+** | Ground Swell | Powerful, well-organized waves. Long intervals between sets. Best wave quality. |
| **10-14s** | Long Period | Good quality waves with consistent sets. Solid surf conditions. |
| **7-10s** | Wind Swell | Locally generated waves. Average quality, more frequent but less organized. |
| **<7s** | Short Chop | Poor wave quality. Disorganized, choppy conditions. |

### Why Period Matters More Than You Think

A 1-meter wave at 14 seconds has **significantly more energy** than a 1-meter wave at 7 seconds. The longer period swell:
- Travels faster and farther
- Creates more powerful waves
- Produces longer, more rideable faces
- Results in more consistent sets

## Ground Swell vs Wind Swell

### Ground Swell (14s+)
- Generated by distant storms, often thousands of kilometers away
- Waves are well-organized with clean lines
- Long intervals between sets
- More powerful and consistent
- Better for quality surfing

### Wind Swell (<10s)
- Generated locally by nearby winds
- Waves are choppier and less organized
- Shorter intervals between waves
- Less powerful but more frequent
- Can still be fun in the right conditions

### Mixed Conditions
When both ground swell and wind swell are present (bimodal sea state), you'll see both swells listed in the forecast. The combined wave height is calculated using energy addition.

## Wave Steepness: Clean or Choppy?

Wave steepness determines how organized and rideable the waves are.

**Calculation:** Steepness = Wave Height / Wavelength

### Steepness Categories

| Category | Steepness | Conditions |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Swell** | < 0.025 | Clean, lined-up waves. Best for surfing. |
| **Average** | 0.025-0.04 | Moderate organization. Good conditions. |
| **Steep** | 0.04-0.055 | Steeper waves, less organized. |
| **Very Steep** | > 0.055 | Choppy, disorganized. Difficult conditions. |

Lower steepness = smoother, more rideable waves. Ground swells naturally have lower steepness than wind swells.

## Wave Power Indicator

Our **Wave Power** indicator shows the energy in the waves, not just the height.

**Why it matters:** Same height, different power:
- 1m @ 14s = High power (ground swell)
- 1m @ 7s = Low power (wind swell)

### Power Levels

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Epic** | Maximum energy. Powerful, fast-moving waves. |
| **High** | Strong energy. Good push and speed. |
| **Medium** | Moderate energy. Standard conditions. |
| **Low** | Low energy. May feel weak or mushy. |

## Set Waves & Sneaker Sets

Understanding set wave patterns helps you stay safe and catch the best waves.

### Statistical Breakdown

Based on Significant Wave Height (Hs):

| Type | Size | Frequency |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Average Sets** | Hs | Most waves |
| **Occasional Sets** | ~1.3x Hs | Top 10% of waves |
| **Sneaker Sets** | ~1.7x Hs | Top 1% ("wave of the day") |

### Example
If the forecast is 1m (3.3ft):
- Average sets: ~1m (3.3ft)
- Occasional sets: ~1.3m (4.3ft)
- Sneaker sets: up to ~1.7m (5.6ft)

**Always watch for bigger waves!** Never turn your back on the ocean.

## Forecast Confidence

Weather models become less accurate the further into the future they predict.

### Confidence by Time Horizon

| Timeframe | Confidence | Use For |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **0-24 hours** | 95% - Very reliable | Planning today's session |
| **1-3 days** | 85% - Reliable | Planning your week |
| **3-5 days** | 70% - Fairly accurate | General planning |
| **5-7 days** | 55% - General trend | Looking ahead |
| **7+ days** | 35% - Indicative only | Very rough idea |

**Pro tip:** Check forecasts daily as conditions approach. The closer to the day, the more accurate the prediction.

## Wave Consistency

Our **Consistency** indicator tells you how predictable the wave heights will be.

### Consistency Levels

| Level | Variance | What to Expect |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Very Consistent** | ±12% | Ground swell. Predictable sets, easy timing. |
| **Consistent** | ±20% | Good organization. Reliable conditions. |
| **Variable** | ±30% | Wind swell mixing. Harder to predict sets. |
| **Inconsistent** | ±40% | Choppy conditions. Unpredictable wave sizes. |

## Break Types & Wave Transformation

Waves transform as they approach the shore. Different break types behave differently:

### Beach Breaks
- Sandy bottom with shifting sandbars
- Wave shape varies with sand movement
- Multiple peaks possible
- Good for beginners (usually)

### Reef Breaks
- Coral or rock reef bottom
- Consistent wave shape and breaking point
- Can be shallow - know the depth!
- More predictable once you know the spot

### Point Breaks
- Waves wrap around a headland
- Wave focusing effect (waves can be larger than open beach)
- Long rides possible
- Usually one main takeoff zone

## Reading the Forecast

### What Makes a Great Surf Day?

1. **Offshore winds** - Holds up the wave face for clean conditions
2. **Long period swell (10s+)** - More power and better wave quality
3. **Appropriate height** - Matches your skill level
4. **Low steepness** - Clean, organized waves
5. **Consistent sets** - Easier to time your session

### Red Flags

- Onshore winds - Creates choppy, difficult conditions
- Short period (<7s) - Usually poor wave quality
- High steepness - Disorganized, hard to ride
- Very large swells - May close out or be dangerous
