# LINEUP — Design Context

## Product
**LINEUP** is a surf forecast and decision tool for Victorian and Australian surf breaks. It tells surfers whether to go out — "Know when it's on." The core value prop is *decision-first*: no raw data overload, just a clear go/no-go with the reasoning behind it.

## Target Audience
- **Primary**: Active surfers in Victoria, Australia — checking conditions before driving to the beach or while already there (mobile-first)
- **Context of use**: Quick decisions at 6am, on the road to a break, or standing in the carpark
- **Skill range**: Intermediate to experienced surfers who understand swell period, wind direction, and tide effects
- **Mindset**: Time-poor, impatient, wants a verdict — not a data dump

## Core Use Cases
1. **Go/no-go decision** — Is it worth surfing today? Which break?
2. **Break comparison** — Which of the listed Victorian breaks is best right now?
3. **Session planning** — 10-day forecast to pick the best upcoming window
4. **Conditions deep-dive** — Wave height, period, swell direction, wind, tides for a specific break
5. **Favorite tracking** — Quick access to the breaks they surf most

## Brand Personality
- **Tone**: Authoritative but not stiff. Like a knowledgeable local giving you the honest call.
- **Voice**: Decisive, direct, surfer-literate. Uses surf vocabulary ("offshore", "set wave", "ground swell") without explaining it.
- **Feel**: Functional and sharp — like a professional tool, not a consumer app. Closer to a weather instrument than a travel guide.
- **NOT**: Tourist-friendly, overly polished, flowery. This is a utility for people who know what they're doing.

## Design Principles
1. **Decision first** — The verdict (Go now / Keep an eye on it / Wait it out) is always the most prominent element
2. **Data in service of action** — Numbers should support the call, not BE the call. Score → decision → reasoning.
3. **Mobile-first at the beach** — Large touch targets, legible in sunlight, fast to scan
4. **Earn trust through precision** — The interface should feel accurate and calibrated, not enthusiastic

## Color System
- **Brand navy** (`#0B1F2A`) — Primary brand color, used for hero cards and decision surfaces
- **Brand ocean** (`#2E8BC0`) — Accent color, links, active states, brand tagline
- **Surf good** (`#4CAF50`) — "Go now" state — green
- **Surf okay** (`#F4D35E`) — "Keep an eye on it" state — amber
- **Surf poor** (`#E63946`) — "Wait it out" state — red

## Typography Direction
- Display: Something with character — condensed or editorial, evocative of coastline/maritime
- Body: Clean and readable at small sizes in poor lighting conditions (beach, outdoors)
- Avoid: System fonts, Inter, Roboto, generic sans-serifs

## Known Design Debt
- Font stack is currently system-ui — needs a proper type system (`/typeset`)
- Card grid is identical cards — needs visual differentiation by score (`/arrange`)
- Hero section uses the "big number metric" template — needs a surf-specific rethink (`/bolder`)
- No dark mode (users check at dawn/dusk, often in low light)

## Technical Context
- Next.js 14 (App Router), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v3
- Data: Open-Meteo (marine/weather), BOM observations, WorldTides
- AI: Claude for surf report generation
- Deployment: Vercel
